Follow these steps to assess biases

Step 1: Define Audience & Intent

Ask:

1. Demographics:

  • "Who is my target audience? (Age, gender, race, culture, abilities, socioeconomic status)"
  • "Are there marginalized groups within this audience that are often overlooked?"

2. Purpose:

  • "What message or emotion should this output convey?"
  • "Could certain representations undermine this intent?"

Action:

  • List specific audience traits (e.g., "Working-class Mexican women aged 30–50").
  • Example prompt refinement:
    ❌ "Generate an image of a teacher."
    ✅ "Generate an image of a middle-aged Afro-Latina teacher in a public school classroom with diverse students."

Step 2: Audit Output for Key Bias Types

Use this checklist to evaluate the AI's output:

A. Representation

  • Inclusion: Are all relevant groups visible?
  • Roles: Are marginalized groups in empowered/non-stereotypical roles?
  • Tokenism: Is diversity superficial (e.g., one Black face in a crowd)?

B. Stereotypes

  • Tropes: Does the output reinforce clichés (e.g., "submissive Asian woman," "disabled person as inspirational")?
  • Beauty Standards: Are Eurocentric features (light skin, straight hair) overrepresented?

C. Context & Authenticity

  • Setting: Does the environment match the audience's reality (e.g., rural vs. urban)?
  • Cultural Accuracy: Are traditions/symbols depicted correctly (e.g., hijab styles, regional clothing)?

D. Language & Framing (For text-based AI)

  • Defaults: Does the text assume heteronormativity, ableism, or gender binaries?
  • Tone: Is language neutral or judgmental (e.g., "fiery Latina" vs. "assertive woman")?

Action:

  • Compare output against your audience list. Flag mismatches (e.g., "AI generated only light-skinned nurses").

Step 3: Refine Prompts with Constraints

Ask:

  • "What explicit instructions can counter biases I found?"
  • "How can I make the AI prioritize authenticity?"

Action:

  • Add directive keywords to prompts:
    ✅ "Generate a corporate team photo with equal gender/racial diversity, including a wheelchair user as CEO."
    ✅ "Write a story about a refugee family, avoiding poverty porn tropes."
  • Exclude stereotypes:
    ❌ "Happy African village."
    ✅ "Modern Nigerian tech entrepreneurs in Lagos."

Step 4: Test & Compare Variations

Ask:

  • "How do different phrasings affect results?"
  • "Does specifying cultural details improve accuracy?"

Action:

  • Run 3–5 prompt variations (e.g., "diverse" vs. "inclusive," "African" vs. "Ghanaian").
  • Example test:
    Prompt 1: "Generate a doctor." → (Output: 80% White men)
    Prompt 2: "Generate a doctor of Somali descent, female, aged 40." → (Output: Accurate representation)

Step 5: Manual Adjustments & Validation

Ask:

  • "Can I edit the output to fix residual biases?"
  • "Would my target audience approve this?"

Action:

  1. Edit directly: Use Photoshop for images, rewrite AI text.
  2. User testing: Share outputs with diverse reviewers. Ask:
    • "Does this feel authentic to your experience?"
    • "What stereotypes do you notice?"

Example:

An AI-generated "Indian wedding" image shows only Hindu rituals. After feedback, adjust prompts to include Muslim/Sikh weddings.
